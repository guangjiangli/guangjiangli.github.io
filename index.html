About me

I'm currently working on Terahertz gyrotron amplifier as a postdoc in Richard Temkin's group in PSFC at Massachusetts Institute of Technology. During my PhD study in Lyubov Titova's group in Worcester Polytechnic Insititute, I focused on photoinduced carrier dynamics in 2D materials (MXenes) and solar energy mateirals using Time resolved Terahertz spectroscopy. I worked as a research assistant at Institute of Solid-State Physics, Chinese Academy of Sciences from 2015 to 2017. I obtained master's degree on condensed matter physics from University of Science and Techonolgy of China in 2017. I have completed my summer intern project in Planck Institute for Polymer, Mainz, Germany in 2018. Outside of lab, I like playing basketball, hiking, swimming. I am also a streetphotographer, I ocassionally shot with film camera.
