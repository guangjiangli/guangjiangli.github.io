About me

I currently work in the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) Department at Brookhaven National Laboratory (BNL), focusing on the design of normal-conductive and superconducting radio-frequency (RF) cavities for both the electron storage ring and the hadron storage ring. From Aug. 2021 to May 2024, I worked in Richard Temkin's group at the Plasma Science and Fusion Center (PSFC) at MIT studying THz gyrotron amplifiers, laser-driven semiconductor switches for THz resonator rings, temperature-dependent permitivity of zirocnia by VNA with THz extenders and the design of THz coupling in the zirconia probe for Dynamic Nuclear Polarization-Nuclear Magnetic Resonance (DNP-NMR). In August 2021, I completed my PhD in Lyubov Titova's group at Worcester Polytechnic Institute, where I focused on photoinduced carrier dynamics in 2D materials (MXenes) and solar energy materials using time-resolved terahertz spectroscopy. Outside of the lab, I enjoy swimming and astro-landscape photography.

Research interests

  • High-power millimeter-wave/sub-terahertz technologies, including advanced source development and quasi-optical systems.
  • Radio-frequency and microwave engineering for particle accelerators and quantum computing
  • Terahertz spectroscopy of materials, including nano- and two-dimensional materials
